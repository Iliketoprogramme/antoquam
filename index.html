What we do

01 —

Record

We conduct oral history interviews with survivors, descendants, and witnesses — preserving voices before they are lost to time.

02 —

Amplify

Through card news, short-form video, and digital storytelling, we bring these histories to a new generation who may never encounter them in a textbook.

03 —

Archive

Every story we collect is preserved in an open digital archive — free, searchable, and accessible to researchers, families, and the public.

Who we are

Five students.
One shared conviction.

We met through National History Day, spent months researching the Bodo League Massacre — a story of over 100,000 civilians that Korea suppressed for decades — and placed 1st at the Korea regional competition.

That experience changed how we see history. Not as a fixed record, but as a collection of choices about whose voices to keep. Antoquam is our answer to that.
